# Winner Tilt AI — Universe Methodology v1.0

**Status:** Frozen  
**Freeze date:** 2026-07-23

## Objective
Maintain a diversified, investable competition set for monthly scoring. The universe is not a buy list.

## Structure
- 96 companies
- 80 Core
- 16 Emerging
- 15 portfolio holdings
- 15 reserve names
- Monthly scoring
- Universe review every 6 months
- Portfolio rebalance every 6 months

## Eligibility
At each six-month review, a security must be listed on Nasdaq or NYSE (including approved liquid ADRs), have at least 252 trading days, sufficient financial data, normal listing status, and adequate liquidity. Standard candidates target market capitalization of at least USD 5 billion; Emerging candidates target at least USD 1 billion.

Eligibility is operational rather than permanently assumed. A frozen constituent can be suspended from scoring if it fails a hard filter at a review date.

## Core vs Emerging
Core companies generally have established scale, more complete financial histories, and clearer business durability. Emerging companies may have higher growth and optionality but receive stricter treatment for cash burn, dilution, execution risk, and position limits.

## Metadata
Each company receives a permanent WT_ID, ticker, company name, exchange, country, group, business stage, quality tier, themes, economic exposure, pool, status, review dates, and inclusion rationale.

## Concentration controls
Sector labels alone are insufficient. Portfolio construction must also control shared economic exposures such as AI capex, cloud spending, power demand, consumer spending, drug pipelines, defense budgets, and commodity prices.

## Change governance
A constituent may be removed for acquisition, delisting, persistent hard-filter failure, severe governance problems, unavailable data, or loss of economic relevance. Price weakness or a low score alone is not a removal reason.

Changes require a versioned decision-log entry. The next scheduled universe review creates v1.1 or v2.0 depending on materiality.
